Receive Rappi orders in your OMS, track the order lifecycle in real time, and integrate webhooks, polling, and operational events for Lite or Full modes.

Getting started with the Orders API

What is Open Orders?

Open Orders lets retailers receive Rappi orders in their own systems (OMS/ERP) instead of relying only on internal platform screens.

You keep your logistics and operations for picking and delivery, while staying aligned with the same catalog and inventory governance you already use with Rappi.

The integration is real-time: new orders and status changes flow as events between Rappi and your backend. Scheduled orders may arrive early, helping spread operational load across the day.

Is Open Orders suitable for your operation?

Before starting integration, it is important to assess whether your operation has the necessary structure to support the Open Orders model. Consider the following points:

  • Does your company already have a functional inventory integration with Rappi?
  • Is store inventory control efficient enough to minimize stockouts?
  • Can your POS/ERP system process orders from external channels?
  • Is your operations team ready for time-sensitive actions to ensure orders are fulfilled correctly?
  • Do you have a development team (in-house or outsourced) able to implement, maintain, and support the Rappi integration?
🚧 Important: The Open Orders team ships new capabilities regularly to improve the in-store experience. As an integrated partner, you should roll out those updates within 8 weeks of release.

Integration Checklist

Before moving to endpoints, validate these points with the product, operations, and engineering team:

  • Map the lifecycle of the order and the impact of each event on the app (end customer and courier).
  • Translate internal events from your OMS (WMS/POS) to Rappi events and vice versa.
  • List ERP/picking limitations for exceptions (stockouts, swaps, partial cancellation) and define plan B.
  • ImplementWebhookidempotent (retries, signature if applicable, logs) for ingestion of orders.
  • Ensure that any relevant operational change is reported back to Rappi in Full (and confirm ACK when required).

Operation models (Lite and Full)

Open Orders offers two modes. The choice defines who initiates status changes and which API actions are available.

Orders Lite

In Lite, Rappi runs picking on its own solutions. Your system receives notifications and tracks the order, but does not change the operational flow via the Open Orders API.

Compatible with Rappi picking (e.g.: Shopper App, Mi Tienda, RT Picking, Cashless).

Orders Full

In Full, the retailer operates in its own OMS and sends events back to Rappi (picking, invoice, courier, etc.). There is more autonomy — and more responsibility to stay in sync.

Not compatiblewith native picking from Rappi: your operation is responsible for the separation.

Understanding the Order Lifecycle

Order Overview

All orders at Rappi are treated as a sequential flow of events. A common request goes through different stages from its creation to delivery to the end customer.

Flow events are divided into two main types:

Rappi Events

These are events generated directly by the Rappi platform during order processing.

  • order_created— Notifies the creation of a new order and sends all information related to it.
  • courier_assigned— Informs that a delivery person (RT) has been assigned to the order and he's on his way to the store.
  • order_finished— Indicates that the order was successfully delivered to the customer end.

Retailer Events

These are events generated by the retailer's order management system (OMS) or picking solution used in the operation.

  • start_picking— Notifies that the store team has started separating the order products.
  • report_invoice— Informs that the separation has been completed and that the tax documentation (NF or receipt) is now available according to your process.
  • request_courier— Requests Rappi to begin the process of attributing delivery person for the order.

Order Receiving

Rappi provides two main ways for retailers to receive orders and updates on time actual:

  • Order Event Webhook
  • Polling Endpoint

These methods can be used separately or together, ensuring greater reliability in receiving events.

Method What is When to use
Webhook Rappi sends a POST to your URL when there is a new event. As the main channel — lower latency and a better operational experience.
Polling Your system periodically consults the Rappi API. As contingency (queues, network failures, reprocessing, audits).

Order Event Webhook

By configuring a Webhook for Rappi orders, your operation can receive orders automatically as soon as they are created on the platform.

The order is created through a request POST sent to the URL configured by retailer, using the endpoint:

Webhook (URL configured by partner) 
POST https://your-url.com/orders

The order identifier (order_id) is not sent in the URL, but in the body of the request.

The sent payload contains all relevant order information, including:

  • Store details
  • Customer Information
  • Delivery address
  • Products and combos
  • Payment method
  • Delivery person information
  • Order status
  • Creation date
  • Events related to the order

Scheduled Orders — field place_at

The field place_at is used to identify scheduled requests.

NOW Orders

When the order is not scheduled, the field will be sent empty or null:

Example (JSON) 
"place_at": ""

or

Example (JSON) 
"place_at": null

Scheduled Orders

For scheduled orders, the field will contain the scheduled date and time:

Example (JSON) 
"place_at": "2025-04-03T12:00:00Z"

Personally Identifiable Information

When necessary, personal information — such as CPF — can be sent in the field identification.number, captured directly in the app's checkout flow.

Polling Endpoint

The polling method allows you to proactively consult active requests and events on the platform Rappi.

When making a request GET to the endpoint below, all requests will be returned pending issues not yet confirmed by the retailer:

Pending orders 
GET {country_url}/api/open-api/v1/orders

It is also possible to consult a specific order using:

Order by ID 
GET {country_url}/api/open-api/v1/orders/{order_id}

The return will follow exactly the same JSON format sent by the Webhook.

Request Limit (Rate Limit)

By default, retailers have a limit of:

100 requests per minute

This limit can be adjusted upon request to Rappi.

Order Status

The field status present in both the Webhook and the polling endpoint indicates the current step of the request within the operational flow.

Possible values are:

Status Description
created Active order, awaiting separation or in the process of separation
invoice_reported Separation completed and final basket of products defined
finished Order delivered to the end customer
canceled Order canceled by Rappi or the retailer

Which combination to use in practice?

The choice depends on your OMS and observability maturity (logs, alerts, queues). The recommendation of Rappi is objective:

  • Webhook as the main mechanism for receiving events
  • Polling as a complementary contingency mechanism

If an event does not arrive via webhook, polling helps detect gaps and recover state without relying only on blind retries.

Order Management

There are two order management models available on Open Orders:

FULL

In the modelFULL, the retailer uses its own tools and systems (OMS) to carry out the separation of orders.

In this scenario, the retailer is responsible for sending Rappi updates about each stage of the operational process.

LITE

In the modelLITE, order separation is carried out using the solutions available by Rappi itself.

In this case, updates on operational events are sent by the platform itself to the retailer.

Order Delivered

As with the delivery driver assignment process, the order completion is also sent via POST for the configured Webhook.

When the order is completed:

  • the field status takes on the value finished
  • the field delivered_at receive the delivery date and time
Example (JSON) 
{
  "delivered_at": "2023-02-10T12:34:07Z"
}

Events at Open Orders FULL

This section applies to operations on the modelFull: you confirm what you received from Rappi, updates order status in the API and finishes with a handshake when the courier picks up at the store.

Suggested reading order:event acknowledgment (ACK) → status update (picking / NF / partial cancellations) → delivery person request → handshake.

Order Lifecycle — Orders FULL

The Orders FULL model was developed for operations that use their own picking solution (picking). In this scenario, the store team manages orders directly in the retailer's system (OMS), from receipt to delivery to the Rappi delivery person.

Features of Orders FULL

This integration requires the retailer to have:

  • Your own order management system (OMS)
  • An in-house picking solution
  • Teams trained to view, process and update orders in real time

In this model, the retailer is responsible for sending Rappi updates regarding the order's operational events.

Event Confirmation (Acknowledgement)

All events sent by Rappi must be confirmed by the retailer. This ensures that the order has been received and processed correctly.

There are two ways of confirmation:

Implicit Confirmation

Implicit confirmation occurs through the Webhook response. Whenever the retailer responds to the Webhook with a successful HTTP code (200 to 299), the event will automatically be considered confirmed.

Example of a valid response:

Example (JSON) 
{
  "status": 200
}

or simply:

Example (JSON) 
{}

Explicit Confirmation

Explicit confirmation is used when:

  • There is no Webhook configured
  • Webhook fails
  • HTTP response is not received correctly

In this case, you must manually send a request to the confirmation endpoint.

Confirmation endpoint

ACK 
POST {country_url}/api/open-api/v1/orders/acknowledge

Payload

Payload (JSON) 
{
  "acknowledgments": [
    {
      "order_id": 1234567,
      "event_uuid": "00000000-0000-0000-0000-000000000000"
    }
  ]
}
📘 If an event has already been confirmed previously, the API will return the error not_found_acknowledgements with status406 Not Acceptable.

Event UUID

Each event generated by Rappi has a unique identifier:event_uuid.

For calls that change the order, always send the latest UUID in the header so Rappi knows you are reacting to the current state, not an older event.

Event Order Status Identification
Order Created created First notification
Product Modifications created Array changes products
Courier Assigned created or invoice_reported Object fields courier filled
Order Canceled canceled Field status
Order Finished finished Field status

The eventsOrder CanceledandOrder Finishedclose the order flow and do not need to be confirmed.

Unconfirmed Events

Some events are considered critical to the operation.

If Rappi identifies that an important event was not confirmed within the configured time (for example, pattern,20 minutes), the order may be canceled automatically.

The main critical events are:

  • order_created
  • Product modification events
  • remove_product
  • remove_product_units

Order Status Update

Upon receipt of the order, the retailer must update the operational progress through the endpoints appropriate.

Beginning of Separation (start_picking)

This event must be sent when the store team starts sorting the products.

Endpoint

Endpoint 
POST {country_url}/api/open-api/v1/orders/{order_id}/start_picking

Payload

Payload (JSON) 
{
  "picking_time": 15
}

The field picking_time represents the estimated separation time in minutes. If it does not exist available estimate, use the default of 15 minutes.

Finalization of the Separation (report_invoice)

Indicates picking is complete and that the invoice or receipt is available according to your fiscal process.

Endpoint

Endpoint 
POST {country_url}/api/open-api/v1/orders/{order_id}/report_invoice

Payload

Payload (JSON) 
{
  "total_price": 12.5,
  "image_url": "image.com"
}

Observations

  • The decimal separator must be "."
  • Do not use thousands separator
  • The field image_url is optional
🚧 After sending the report_invoice, the product basket is locked and will no longer be subject to changes.

Order Cancellation

If it is necessary to cancel an order after it has been created, use the endpoint below:

Endpoint

Endpoint 
POST {country_url}/api/open-api/v1/orders/{order_id}/cancel

Payload

Payload (JSON) 
{
  "type": "reason",
  "details": {}
}

Product Removal (Partial Cancellation)

During separation, products may be unavailable in stock.

Total Product Removal— when there are no units of the product available.

Endpoint 
DELETE {country_url}/api/open-api/v1/orders/{order_id}/products/{product_id}

The endpoint accepts empty body.

Partial Removal of Units— when there is less quantity than requested by the customer.

Endpoint 
PATCH {country_url}/api/open-api/v1/orders/{order_id}/products/{product_id}/quantity

Payload for unit products

Payload (JSON) 
{
  "units": 2
}

Payload for products by weight

Payload (JSON) 
{
  "grams": 250
}
🚧 Removing all products from the order will result in automatic order cancellation. Additionally, remove More than 50% of items may require intervention from Rappi support.

Product Substitutions

Currently, the Open Orders FULL model does not have native support for product substitutions. If your operation depends on this flow, it will be necessary to use another picking solution.

Delivery Boy Assignment

To continue the logistics flow, it is necessary to request a Rappi delivery person.

Delivery Person Request (request_courier)

Endpoint 
POST {country_url}/api/open-api/v1/orders/{order_id}/request_courier

Payload

Payload (JSON) 
{
  "store_id": 10,
  "vehicle_type": "car"
}

Available vehicle types

  • motorbike
  • car
  • bicycle
🚧 Requesting the delivery person too early can increase waiting time in the store and negatively impact sales operational indicators.

Event courier_assigned

When the delivery person is assigned, Rappi will send an update via Webhook filling in the object courier of the request.

Example (JSON) 
"courier": {
  "phone": "18003334444",
  "last_name": "Johnson",
  "first_name": "Jane",
  "vehicle_type": "motorbike"
}

Handshake Process

The handshake is the validation process used to confirm that the correct delivery person is picking up the request.

Applies to flowFullafter picking, invoice and delivery driver assignment — it is the last barrier before releasing the merchandise into the store.

Handshake Request

Endpoint

Endpoint 
POST {country_url}/api/open-api/v1/orders/{order_id}/handshake

Answer

Response (JSON) 
{
  "codes": [
    "033903",
    "286928",
    "788069"
  ],
  "expires_at": "2021-06-18T16:42:03Z"
}

Only one of the codes is valid and will be sent to the delivery person in the Rappi app.

Handshake Rules

The handshake should only be initiated when:

  • The order is completely separate
  • The invoice has been issued
  • A delivery person is assigned to the order
  • The delivery person is already present in the store

Additionally:

  • Codes expire in5 minutes
  • After expiration, new codes need to be generated

Handshake Validation

Endpoint

Endpoint 
POST {country_url}/api/open-api/v1/orders/{order_id}/handshake/validate

Payload

Payload (JSON) 
{
  "code": "788069"
}

If the code is correct:

  • The API will return status204
  • The order can be handed over to the delivery person

Otherwise, an error will be returned400containing:

  • New codes available
  • Expiration time
  • Remaining number of attempts

Main Error Codes

Code Status Description
invalid_handshake_code 400 Invalid handshake code
order_not_found 404 Order not found
invalid_status 409 Current status does not allow operation
handshake_already_started 208 Handshake process already started
no_validation_retries_left 406 Attempt limit exceeded
no_courier_assigned 406 No delivery person assigned
product_not_found_in_order 404 Product not found in order
price_difference_exceeds_percentage_limit 400 Price difference above the permitted limit
substitutions_not_configured 403 Overrides not configured

Events in Open Orders LITE

Order Lifecycle — Orders LITE

The Orders LITE model was developed for retailers who want predominantly integration informative or that use specific picking and checkout solutions.

Unlike the FULL model, Orders LITE does not allow operational changes to orders through the API, functioning mainly as a query and monitoring mechanism for events generated by the Rappi.

Features of Orders LITE

Orders LITE is compatible with different solutions provided by Rappi, including:

  • Shopper App
  • Mi Store
  • RT Picking
  • Cashless

In this model:

  • It is possible to use Webhooks to receive order notifications
  • It is possible to consult orders via polling
  • It is not allowed to update, cancel or modify orders via the API

In addition to the Webhook, there is a polling endpoint for proactive querying. Each country has a different base URL; is Mandatory to authenticate before consuming the endpoints.

Returns pending and unconfirmed orders from the authenticated retailer.

Cancellation Reference

Basic Cancellation Structure

To cancel an order in Open Orders, use the following endpoint:

Endpoint 
POST {country_url}/api/open-api/v1/orders/{order_id}/cancel

Example using cURL:

cURL 
curl --location 'https://{COUNTRY}/api/open-api/v1/orders/{YOUR_ORDER}/cancel' \
--header 'Authorization: Bearer {YOUR_TOKEN}' \
--header 'event_uuid: {LAST_EVENT_UUID}' \
--header 'Content-Type: application/json' \
--data '{
  "type": "technical_issues",
  "details": {}
}'

Payload Structure

Field Description
type Reason for cancellation
details Additional information related to the reason
📘 For most reasons,details can be {}. Some types require additional information.
